Acquisition Trends

Weekly Acquisition Trend by Platform

üí° Trend Analysis

Week 41 (Oct 6-12) was the strongest acquisition week with 3,278 new users. There's a clear declining trend starting in Week 42, dropping to 1,658 users in Week 43, and 1,140 in Week 44. iOS consistently outpaces Android by roughly 1.7:1 ratio across all weeks.

üí° Paid Ads Campaign Context

Week 41's strength is directly attributable to paid advertising support. On October 7th, the majority of paid campaigns were turned off, leaving only the best-performing campaigns on TikTok and Meta. On October 8th, Meta, TikTok, and Apple Search Ads were completely shut down. Finally, on October 15th, the remaining Google Ads campaign was turned off. The sharp decline in organic user acquisition following Week 41 demonstrates how paid advertising efforts create a halo effect that drives organic growth‚Äîwhen paid campaigns stopped, organic acquisition declined by 46-47% week-over-week.

Key Recommendations

üéØ Improve Activation Performance

With 27.56% activation rate (users completing at least one key action), there's significant room for improvement. Focus on converting the 72.44% of users who sign up but don't complete important revenue-generating actions. Implement targeted onboarding flows and incentives to drive first transactions.

üí∞ Leverage Texas Market Dominance

Texas represents 24.5% of organic users. Invest in localized marketing, partnerships, and community building in Houston, Dallas, and other major Texas cities to maximize this stronghold.

üì± Optimize iOS Experience

iOS users comprise 63.45% of organic growth. Prioritize iOS feature development, App Store optimization, and iOS-specific marketing campaigns to capitalize on this platform preference.

‚ö° Address Declining Trend

The 32.7% week-over-week drop (Week 42 to 43) signals concern. The data clearly shows paid advertising's halo effect on organic growth. Consider strategic paid campaigns or seasonal promotions to revitalize organic acquisition.

üöÄ Critical Engagement Problem

Only 0.46% of users are highly engaged (10+ actions), while 71.87% never complete a single revenue action. An additional 17.28% complete only 1 action and churn. This indicates a severe activation and retention problem. Urgently implement onboarding optimization, push notifications, and first-transaction incentives.

üéÅ Expand Feature Adoption Beyond Cards

Card Activation leads at 24.49%, but P2P (4.80%), Remittances (1.70%), and TopUp (1.06%) lag significantly. Implement in-app tutorials, progressive onboarding, and first-use bonuses to drive adoption of revenue-generating features.
